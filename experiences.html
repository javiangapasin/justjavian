CCS IT Consultant (Summer and Fall 2025)

Introduction

This was my first Co-op work term during my undergraduate degree. During this introductory term, I worked as a Student IT Consultant with the University’s Computing and Communication Services (CCS). This was an 8 month term, but it started off with this summer term, and so this experience will be split up between May and August, and September to December. These work terms were really fun and an amazing learning experience, and I hope this portion of my website can reflect that.

An Overview of Computing and Communication Services (CCS)

The University of Guelph’s Computing and Communication Services, or more popularly known as CCS, is the core IT service that the campus operates on. There are a wide variety of information technology operations that CCS supports and runs, including the following:

  • Gryph Mail, Office 365, and Microsoft Application Support
  • Central Login Account Creation and Deletion
  • Information Security and Identity and Access Management
  • Web Development and Solutions
  • Software Licensing and Distribution
  • Networking and Telecommunication Services
  • Just about any other tech problem you can think of!

My Work at CCS

I was stationed in the IT Help Centre portion of CCS. The Help Centre was mainly responsible for being the front-line support of CCS. We would receive a major portion of the initial emails, and from there we would give our best effort to solve the issue. If we weren’t able to solve it, we would then have to deduce which of CCS’ vast teams would be able to help with the issue. Alongside frontline support, the Help Centre specialized in assisting students with things like:

  • Password resets for their central login accounts
  • Multi-factor authentication resets
  • Helping unlock accounts
  • Initial troubleshooting for Wifi issues, software downloads, and telephone features
  • General knowledge of the school's operations and services

Because a lot of the role was very best effort oriented, it meant having to have good general knowledge of the IT operations and practices on campus, and a good understanding of how most of the technology we used works. A big portion of this term stemmed from in person help as well. I'd often help out at the Ask-Us Desk in the Library in order to provide live and face to face IT support which made a lot of the issues easier and quicker to solve!

Summer 2025 Term (May - August)

This was my first Co-op work term during my undergraduate degree. This summer work term was really fun and an amazing learning experience. I was nervous at first, but I quickly adapted to the role and was able to contribute meaningfully to the team. I set out to fulfill several goals during this term, which I will outline below.

Goals Throughout the Term

Building Technical Confidence

One of my biggest goals this term and in this role was just to become more independent and efficient at diagnosing IT issues. During the beginning of the term, I stuck to simpler and easier tickets regarding password resets and MFA resets. As time went on, I was able to intentionally take on more challenging tickets regarding weird account creations, networking issues, or tougher Microsoft installation errors that required the help of other teams along with my own experimenting.

I grew a lot by relying on our CCS Sharepoint and internal knowledge base. I’d first look through our Sharepoint to see if there was anything I could find that was related to the issue I was facing. I’d also use keywords and search them up in our ticketing system. I think the best way I learned was to experiment and try to replicate the issue myself and try and work through a solution as if I was the client, but using that knowledge that I had already had and then gained.

Becoming Fluent in Technology Translation

Something I really wanted to improve on was being able to even better explain more complex technological issues to people who might not be as tech savvy, so I tried to work a good amount on my own written and verbal communication in that regard. I helped in documentation, where I updated and created our IT Help articles, making sure the steps were easy and simple to follow for people of all technical levels. I made sure to think like I was the user in order to understand what issues they might run into, and I created and sort of focused my documentation to follow along with what a user might do or run into.

On a daily basis, while speaking with clients over the phone or in person, I also learned and improved on how to explain technical problems to clients in plain language. This quite often meant slowing down and avoiding very technical jargon, or finding ways to relate the concept to something that they were already familiar with. It quite literally was like translating a language!

Expanding my Technical Literacy

This role gave me a lot of hands-on experience with many of the core tools, systems, and processes used at our University, from Gryph Mail and the Office 365 suite, to helping troubleshoot things like phishing emails, network and phone issues, as well as account issues. Instead of just memorizing solutions and processes and the way things work, I also wanted to understand why these types of problems happened in the first place. This would help myself in the future and generally just help me be able to better troubleshoot these issues for clients and myself as I progress in my career.

This determination to more deeply understand the root cause of each problem and to keep expanding my technical literacy genuinely paid off. At the end of this first term, I truly feel a lot more confident in my ability to diagnose and troubleshoot issues faster as well as explain these underlying causes to different people.

Growing Through Teamwork

Aside from technology, a goal that I always have is to continue to learn how to work in a group with other people of all different backgrounds and views. One of the biggest highlights this term was working with such a collaborative, supportive, and diverse team. Our team ranged from Computer Science to just regular Science students, and we all had different backgrounds and experiences. Nonetheless, I bonded really well with the team and made great friends with a lot of them. Whether it was helping someone with a more complex ticket or getting guidance, I always felt like we were a team and in it together.

I usually tried to make a point of offering help when things were busy and sharing whatever I had learned, whenever I could. In return, I learned a lot from my teammates and my supervisors, both technically and in behavior in terms of handling different interactions with clients. I’m happy to call my coworkers my friends and they turned this role into a very positive experience.

Fall 2025 Term (September - December)

This was my second Co-op work term during my undergraduate degree, and my second of two work terms with CCS. This term was the first experience I had in a full operating semester with all of Guelph’s students back on campus, both new and returning. This was also the first term where I got to meet the rest of the Help Centre team who were helping out part time at the Help Desk, so I was excited to see where this one was headed!

Goals Throughout the Term

Strengthening Real-Time Communication

This semester, with students fully back on campus, support demand increased significantly, especially at the start of term when many new students were getting familiar with university systems. I made it a goal to strengthen my real-time communication skills by taking on more phone and in-person tickets. These interactions often required me to explain technical steps verbally without visual aids, particularly when users struggled to follow digital instructions.

Through this experience, I became more confident adjusting my tone, pacing, and explanations based on the client’s level of technical comfort. I learned to check for understanding more frequently and simplify my language when needed. As a result, issues were more often resolved in a single interaction, and I felt more confident communicating clearly under pressure.

Deepening My Understanding of IT Systems

Rather than focusing only on resolving tickets, I wanted to better understand how the university’s IT systems work together. I made a habit of asking why issues occurred and tracing them back to their root causes, especially during high-volume periods early in the semester. This helped me build a clearer understanding of how systems like login services, Office 365, Gryph Mail, and OneDrive are interconnected.

Over time, this deeper understanding improved both my troubleshooting speed and accuracy. I became more confident explaining system behaviour to clients and teammates, which helped reduce confusion and improve support outcomes. This approach strengthened my technical literacy and problem-solving skills throughout the term.

Integrating Technical Knowledge with User Perspective

A key goal for me this term was improving how I combined technical information with user perspective in both client interactions and documentation. I regularly reflected on what explanations were most effective and where users tended to get confused. This helped guide how I structured my responses and support materials.

I also took the lead on updating several documentation websites assigned by my supervisor, ensuring the content was technically accurate while remaining easy to follow. By blending internal resources with personal troubleshooting experience, I was able to create clearer documentation and client responses that resulted in fewer follow-up questions.

Developing Awareness of Digital Equity and Accessibility

This term highlighted how differently students and staff experience technology based on their resources and familiarity with university systems. Many clients relied on older devices, had limited internet access, or were new to using institutional tools, especially at the beginning of the semester. These interactions encouraged me to reflect more carefully on accessibility and digital equity.

As a result, I adapted my support approach by offering alternative solutions, simplifying explanations, and being more patient with troubleshooting steps. These changes helped me provide more inclusive support and better meet the needs of under-resourced or accessibility-challenged users.

Growing as a Team Contributor and Leader

Throughout the term, I focused on becoming a stronger team contributor and developing leadership skills. After discovering a way to track ticket volume, I was motivated to take initiative and handle a higher number of tickets. I also helped train new co-op students by walking them through systems, workflows, and common troubleshooting scenarios.In addition to my technical and leadership contributions, participating in team events such as our holiday gathering helped strengthen relationships across the team and reinforced a collaborative, supportive work environment. This was a great way to finish our term and the atmosphere was great!

In addition, I worked closely with part-time staff at the desk and built strong working relationships that turned into friendships. I also took the lead on several supervisor-assigned projects, including updating documentation and internal websites. These experiences helped me grow as a dependable, proactive team member and strengthened my confidence in taking on leadership responsibilities.

Conclusions

Looking back across both of my work terms, what stands out most is how much I’ve grown not just technically, but personally and professionally. When I first started, I was focused on learning the basics and becoming comfortable in the role. Over time, I’ve developed the confidence to take on more complex issues, explain systems more clearly, and work more independently. Seeing that progression across both terms has been incredibly rewarding and has shown me how much consistent learning and experience can add up.

These roles strengthened my technical skills, but they also reinforced that IT support is fundamentally people-focused. I’ve learned to be more patient, empathetic, and adaptable in my interactions, especially when supporting users with different levels of technical experience or access to resources. Working closely with my teammates, training new co-op students, and building strong relationships with both full-time and part-time staff helped me grow as a communicator and a team member.

Overall, these work terms confirmed my interest in IT and gave me a strong foundation to build on moving forward. I feel more confident in my abilities, more comfortable taking initiative, and better prepared to handle both technical challenges and human-centered problem solving. I’m proud of the growth I’ve achieved and grateful for the experiences and relationships that made it possible.
